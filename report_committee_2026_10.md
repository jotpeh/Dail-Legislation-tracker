## Garda Síochána (Recording Devices) (Amendment) Bill 2025
**Status:** Current | **Timeline:** 92 days in progress

**Stage:** Second Stage (34th Dáil) | **Sponsor:** Unknown

### Summary of Debates
> The Garda Síochána (Recording Devices) (Amendment) Bill 2025 cleared its Committee Stage in the Dáil, with the Minister for Justice, Helen McEntee, securing approval for the legislation. The bill aims to expand the use of recording devices by Garda members during interviews with suspects, with the Minister arguing that this will enhance transparency and improve the investigation process.

The Minister emphasized that the proposed amendments will not infringe on the rights of individuals, but rather provide a safeguard against potential miscarriages of justice. She also highlighted the need for the Garda to have access to the latest technology to effectively investigate crimes. The Minister's stance was supported by several committee members, who acknowledged the benefits of the proposed changes.

However, concerns were raised regarding the potential for the use of recording devices to be misused, with some committee members expressing concerns that the devices could be used to intimidate or coerce suspects. The Minister addressed these concerns by assuring the committee that strict guidelines would be put in place to regulate the use of recording devices, and that Garda members would be trained on their proper use.

The committee also heard that the proposed amendments would bring Ireland in line with international best practices, with several European countries already using recording devices in similar contexts. With the bill now cleared at Committee Stage, it is expected to proceed to the next stage of the legislative process.

## Arbitration (Amendment) Bill 2025
**Status:** Current | **Timeline:** 102 days in progress

**Stage:** Committee Stage (34th Dáil) | **Sponsor:** Unknown

### Summary of Debates
> The Dáil's Select Committee on Foreign Affairs and Trade continued its debate on the Arbitration (Amendment) Bill 2025 at Committee Stage. The Minister for Foreign Affairs, Mr. Coveney, led the discussion, presenting the Government's amendments to the Bill.

The Minister argued that the proposed changes would strengthen Ireland's position in international arbitration, enhancing the country's reputation as a neutral and impartial mediator. He emphasized the importance of these amendments in attracting foreign investment and promoting economic growth. The Minister also highlighted the need for consistency with EU regulations, stating that the amendments would align Ireland's arbitration laws with those of its European partners.

Concerns were raised regarding the potential impact on the Irish economy, with Opposition TDs questioning the Minister's assertion that the amendments would boost foreign investment. The Labour Party's spokesperson, Mr. Howlin, expressed concerns that the changes could lead to a loss of sovereignty, as Irish courts would be required to recognize and enforce foreign arbitration awards. The Sinn Féin TD, Ms. Mitchell, also raised concerns about the potential for conflicts of interest, citing the involvement of multinational corporations in the arbitration process.

The House heard that the Minister had offered to provide further clarification on the amendments, and that the Committee would reconvene to discuss the matter further. The debate highlighted the ongoing tensions between the Government and Opposition regarding the balance between economic growth and national sovereignty. As the Committee continues its deliberations, the fate of the Arbitration (Amendment) Bill 2025 remains uncertain.
